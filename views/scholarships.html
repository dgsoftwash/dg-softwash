🪖 GI Bill Benefits (Your #1 Resource)
💡 Start here first. With a transferred Post-9/11 GI Bill, Virginia public universities are essentially free — tuition, fees, a monthly housing allowance, and $1,000/yr for books. Stack scholarships on top to cover living expenses and private school gaps.
Post-9/11 GI Bill (Chapter 33) — Transferred Benefit
Covers 100% of in-state tuition & fees at any Virginia public university (ODU, VCU, JMU, UVA, Tech, etc.). Also includes a monthly housing stipend (BAH at E-5 w/ dependents) and $1,000/yr book allowance.
100% in-state tuition Housing allowance $1,000/yr books Use within 15 yrs of discharge

Yellow Ribbon Program
If you choose a private school or out-of-state university, the Yellow Ribbon Program can cover the gap above the in-state rate. The school and VA split the difference — can cover full tuition at participating schools.

🏛️ State of Virginia
Virginia Academic Scholarship (VAS)
Merit-based award for high-achieving Virginia public school graduates. Applied through your high school counselor — not a separate application. Up to $3,000/year stackable on top of GI Bill.
Up to $3,000/yr Stackable on GI Bill Via high school counselor
SCHEV Info →
Virginia Guaranteed Assistance Program (VGAP)
Need-based grant for Virginia residents at participating public colleges. Apply via FAFSA — the school awards it automatically if eligible. Covers remaining costs after other aid.
Varies by school Apply via FAFSA

⭐ Military & Veteran-Specific
Survivors' & Dependents' Educational Assistance (DEA / Ch. 35)
Alternative to transferred GI Bill — pays up to ~$1,471/month for full-time enrollment. Can sometimes be better depending on the school. Check both benefits before choosing.
~$1,471/month full-time VA Form 22-5490
