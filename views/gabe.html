📋 Forms & Documents Guide
VA Form 22-1990e — Transfer of Entitlement

What you need before you start

  • Your VA file number or sponsor's Social Security Number
  • Sponsor's branch of service and discharge date (from DD-214)
  • School name, address, and VA certifying official contact
  • Intended major / program of education
  • Anticipated start date

Step-by-step

  • Find your VA file number: Log in to VA.gov → My VA → look for "VA file number" in your profile, or call 1-800-827-1000.
  • Find your school's VA certifying official: Call the financial aid or registrar's office and ask specifically for the "VA certifying official" or "SCO."
  • "Program of education" just means your degree/certificate program (e.g., "B.S. in Computer Science"). Use the catalog name from the school's website.
  • Processing takes 4–6 weeks. Apply as early as possible before the semester starts.
Once approved, you'll get a Certificate of Eligibility (COE). Keep this — you'll need it for Yellow Ribbon and other VA programs.
FAFSA — Free Application for Federal Student Aid

What you need before you start

  • Student's Social Security Number
  • Prior-year federal tax return (use IRS Data Retrieval Tool for auto-fill)
  • Bank account and investment totals (as of the day you file)
  • VA benefit award letter (shows monthly benefit amount)
  • FSA ID — create at StudentAid.gov before you sit down

Key things to know

  • VA benefits are NOT counted as income for the Pell Grant calculation — fill out FAFSA even with GI Bill benefits.
  • Opens October 1 every year for the following academic year. File as early as possible — some state aid (like VGAP) is first-come, first-served.
  • After filing, you'll get a Student Aid Report (SAR) — many scholarship applications require it.
File every year even after freshman year. Aid packages change based on your family's financial situation.
Yellow Ribbon Program

What you need before you start

  • Certificate of Eligibility (COE) from VA — received after approving your 22-1990e
  • School's Yellow Ribbon application form (each school has their own)
  • Copy of 22-1990e approval letter

Step-by-step

  • Confirm 100% entitlement: Yellow Ribbon is only available with 100% Post-9/11 GI Bill eligibility. Verify your COE shows 100%.
  • Request your COE online: Download from VA.gov → Education Benefits, or call 1-888-442-4551 if not received within 6 weeks.
  • Apply early — seats are limited: Schools set their own caps. Apply as soon as you have your COE.
Not all schools participate. Check the VA's Yellow Ribbon school search tool at va.gov before committing to a school.
General Scholarship Applications — Standard Checklist

Documents to prepare

  • Official transcripts — request from your high school registrar at least 2 weeks before deadlines.
  • 2 recommendation letters — ask teachers, coaches, or counselors at least 6 weeks before the deadline.
  • Personal essay — most scholarships ask for 200–500 words. Draft early and have someone proofread.
  • FAFSA Student Aid Report (SAR) — downloaded from StudentAid.gov after filing FAFSA.
  • Proof of military affiliation — copy of sponsor's DD-214 or military ID.

Tips

  • Keep a folder with all documents so you can reuse them across multiple applications.
  • Apply for everything you qualify for — $500–$1,000 scholarships add up and are less competitive.
